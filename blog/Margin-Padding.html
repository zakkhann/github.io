Margin & Padding

What is the difference between Margin, Border, and Padding? In CSS, a margin is the space around an element's border, while padding is the space between an element's border and the element's content. Put another way, the margin property controls the space outside an element, and the padding property controls the space inside an element.

Margin, border, and padding are fundamental concepts in CSS that define the layout and spacing of elements. They are part of the box model, which is a fundamental structure in CSS that represents how elements are laid out in terms of content, padding, border, and margin. Here's a brief explanation of each: Margin: The margin is the space outside an element's border. It clears an area outside the border and does not have any background color. It is used to create space between elements. It doesn't influence the size or layout of the element itself but affects the positioning of the element in relation to other elements.

Border: The border is the actual boundary of the element. It is placed between the padding and the margin. It has properties like color, style, and width that can be specified. It directly influences the dimensions of the element.

Padding: The padding is the space between the content of the element and its border. It clears an area around the content inside the element. It affects the element's size by increasing the space between the content and the border.

Margin is the space outside the border. Border is the actual boundary of the element. Padding is the space between the content and the border.
